The Kiwi School System

Everything you need to know about New Zealand's education system — terms, year levels, and what to expect.

The School Year

A Typical School Year

In New Zealand, the school year runs from February to December and is organised into four 10-week terms. Each term is separated by a 2-week holiday break.

Placement

What Year Will My Child Be In?

If your children are new to New Zealand and have already had some schooling, they will automatically be placed in the same school year as other children of the same age.

Year placement is based on age and birth date. For example, a 10-year-old born in February would be placed in Year 6.

🎂 Starting Age

Children in New Zealand start school when they turn 5 years old. They can begin on their exact fifth birthday — at any point in the school year. Before age 5, children attend kindergarten or daycare.

Daily Life

A Typical School Day

School in New Zealand runs Monday to Friday, and students stay on-site for lunch.

3:00 pm — School finishes, parents pick up
🌇
3:00 – 6:00 pm — After-school care (most schools)

🥗 No Cafeteria!

There are no cafeterias in NZ primary schools. Children must bring their own snacks and lunchbox. This is a common point of surprise for families arriving from France!
