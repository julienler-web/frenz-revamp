About Frenz

Promoting French Bilingual Education Across New Zealand

Frenz (French-English New Zealand) is a non-profit association that has been advocating for French-English bilingual education in New Zealand’s public schools since 1994. We help families find, understand, and access bilingual classes — in both French and English.

Birkdale North School

L’Étoile du Nord — The Northern Star • Established 2012

📍 North Shore, Auckland🏫 Primary (NE–Y6)👨‍🎓 ~200 Students🇫🇷 60% French✅ Open Enrolment

School Overview

The French-English unit at Birkdale North School is called L’Étoile du Nord (The Northern Star) and was established with the help of Frenz in 2012. It is one of Auckland’s most established French bilingual units on the scenic North Shore.

Birkdale North is a publicly funded primary school just minutes from the Harbour Bridge and 20 minutes from downtown Auckland. The school is committed to bilingualism and diversity, also featuring a bilingual Te Reo Māori class, mainstream English classes, and a special needs unit.

The school has about 200 students set in a quiet neighbourhood close to the sea. There is currently no school zone, meaning families from anywhere can enrol year-round. The grounds include large green playing fields, playgrounds, and a swimming pool for Terms 1 and 4.

🇫🇷 Bilingual Class Structure

The NZ curriculum is taught 60% in French and 40% in English. This immersive ratio in the early years builds a strong French foundation while fully achieving all NZ curriculum goals.

Richmond Road School

L’Archipel — The Archipelago • NZ’s oldest French bilingual unit, est. 1994

📍 Ponsonby, Auckland🏫 Primary (NE–Y6)👨‍🎓 ~400 Students⚠️ School Zone Applies

School Overview

L’Archipel at Richmond Road School is New Zealand’s oldest French-English bilingual unit, established with Frenz in 1994. It was the first, and remains one of the most celebrated French bilingual programmes in the country.

Richmond Road is a publicly funded primary school just minutes from downtown Auckland. The school also features bilingual Te Reo Māori and Samoan classes alongside mainstream English classes — a truly vibrant, multicultural community.

With around 400 students, Richmond Road has a school zone and special enrolment scheme. Applications are scored against priority points. The school features junior and senior playgrounds and a large green field.

⚠️ School Zone & Enrolment Scheme

Richmond Road has a school zone. Applications are scored by priority points and must be submitted by September for the following school year. The school does not accept international fee-paying students.

🇫🇷 Bilingual Class Structure

Taught 5 days/week in French for Years 0–2, then alternating: 6 school days in French / 4 days in English for Years 2–6. This structure builds true academic bilingualism across all subjects.

Kowhai Intermediate

La Grande Envolée — The Great Flight • Established 2021 • LabelFrancÉducation Certified

📍 Kingsland, Auckland🏫 Intermediate (Y7–Y8)👨‍🎓 500+ Students🏅 LabelFrancÉducation

School Overview

La Grande Envolée (The Great Flight) at Kowhai Intermediate was established with Frenz in 2021. It supports an important need for children in Years 7–8 to continue their bilingual journey while preparing academically and socially for high school.

Kōwhai Intermediate boasts both a rich history and modern facilities. With 500+ students, it also has bilingual Māori and Samoan units alongside the French programme. Ideally situated in Kingsland near Auckland’s CBD with excellent train and bus connections.

The programme is proudly certified LabelFrancÉducation — the international mark of quality for bilingual French teaching outside France. Many students exceed year-level expectations across all subjects.

NZ curriculum in French 3 days/week, in English 2 days/week. Small class sizes and focused academic development prepare students excellently for secondary school.

School Overview

L’Aurore (The Dawn) is Frenz’s newest and most exciting bilingual unit, opening in 2025 at Haeata Community Campus — developed by Frenz volunteer parents from Burnside Primary, benefiting from 30 years of Frenz experience.

Program Overview

What Is Bilingual Education in NZ?

In New Zealand, bilingual programmes are classes embedded within public primary and intermediate schools where the national curriculum is taught in two languages — French and English. These are not private French schools — they are state schools receiving full Ministry of Education funding, meaning tuition is completely free.

The proportion of French to English instruction varies by school and year level, typically ranging from 50–100% French in the early years, balancing toward an equal split as children grow. The goal is full fluency in both languages by Year 6 or Year 8.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak French to enrol my child?
+
No! Children starting at New Entrant (age 5) do not need any prior French knowledge. The immersive bilingual classroom environment is designed to make language acquisition natural and effortless for young children.
Is bilingual education really free?
+
Yes! All Frenz partner schools are state-funded. Tuition is free for NZ citizens, permanent residents, and eligible work visa holders. A voluntary Frenz membership donation is encouraged but not compulsory.
What is a language evaluation?
+
Schools may conduct a friendly, informal evaluation to assess children’s current French and English levels. This helps place children appropriately. A Frenz representative will guide you through this — it’s not a test to pass or fail!
Can my child join mid-year?
+
This depends on the school and available spaces. Some schools accommodate mid-year enrolments; others follow a strict start-of-year process. Contact us and we’ll advise you on the current situation at your preferred school.
Are there bilingual options for high school?
+
Currently Frenz partners with schools up to Year 8. Various NZ secondary schools offer French language programmes. L’Aurore at Haeata also has potential to extend further. Our team can point you in the right direction.

NZ School Year

New Zealand School Calendar

Term 1

Late Jan – Mid April

The new school year begins. New Entrant children who turn 5 can start anytime. Summer terms with pool activities.

Term 2

Late April – Late June

Autumn/early winter in NZ. Key learning terms. Typically when mid-year enrolments are assessed.

Term 3

Late July – Late Sept

Spring arrives. School camps, sports events. Enrolment applications for the following year open in August.

Term 4

Mid Oct – Mid Dec

End-of-year celebrations, swimming season. Confirmations for the following year sent in November.

🗓️ Key Enrolment Dates

Most schools require applications by August–September for the following year. Language evaluations typically in October. Confirmations by November. Contact Frenz early!

About Frenz

Our story, our mission, and the dedicated volunteers making French bilingual education accessible across New Zealand.

Our Mission

Why We Do What We Do

Frenz (French-English New Zealand) was founded on a simple but powerful belief: every child in New Zealand should have access to high-quality bilingual French-English education in their local public school.

We are a volunteer-led non-profit that bridges the gap between Francophone and Anglophone families and the NZ school system. We guide families, support teachers, advocate for bilingual programmes, and build community around French language and culture in Aotearoa.
