School Overview

Haeata Community Campus

The French-English unit at Haeata Community Campus is called L'Aurore. Frenz helped establish L'Aurore and the first class will open in 2025.

Haeata Community Campus is a publicly funded school in Christchurch's Aranui suburb, just 10 minutes from downtown and a few minutes from New Brighton.

Haeata is an area school, which means that the school caters from new entrants to Year 12. The bilingual programme will welcome students from New Entrants to Year 8.

Campus Life

What Makes Haeata Special

Free Hot Lunches

Haeata offers free school hot lunches to all students, eaten together in the cafeteria—similar to the French "cantine" experience. Children may also bring lunch from home if preferred.

Expansive Facilities

State-of-the-art new buildings with grass playing fields, age-appropriate playgrounds, gymnasium, Art Studio, and Makers Studio (woodwork) available for all classes.

Arts & Culture

Performing arts (music, drama, dance) funded by the school, large auditorium for productions, and weekly Kapa Haka sessions for all classes under Te Ao Māori principles.

Programme Structure

Bilingual Class Structure

50%

French Language

The NZ curriculum is taught in French (50-60% of the time) by a bilingual teacher.

50%

English Language

The remaining 40-50% is taught in English, ensuring balanced bilingual proficiency.

"The goal is that children become proficient in both languages in all academic subjects according to their year group curriculum."

Whilst Haeata's programme is new, it benefits from the Christchurch French community networking since 2017, including collaboration with Alliance Française Christchurch and University of Canterbury French department, plus 30 years of FRENZ national experience.
